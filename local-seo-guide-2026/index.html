Google Business Profile Optimization

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) remains the foundation of local visibility. Getting it right starts with categories.

Primary Category Selection

The single most important ranking factor for your GBP. Research what top competitors use before choosing yours — tools like the Pleper Chrome Extension, Pleper's Category Tool, and GMB Everywhere let you see exactly which categories competitors have selected. Category dilution is a myth: adding relevant secondary categories expands the queries you appear for. Be aware that category edits — even secondary ones — can trigger Google reverification, so have your documentation ready.

Optimize Your Business Name

File a DBA ("doing business as") to legally include keywords. One realtor moved from being filtered out to ranking top-3 across a 50-mile radius after a DBA rebrand. This keeps you compliant with Google's guidelines while capturing keyword relevance.

Extend Your Business Hours

Google ranks businesses higher when they are marked as open. Use a call answering service and list your hours as 24/7. One business doubled its first-time callers within a month of making this change.

Build a Consistent Review Strategy

Track your competitors' monthly review velocity and total count. A steady stream of new reviews signals an active, trustworthy business and directly influences Local Pack placement.

Content Strategy

Stop copying what top-ranking competitors are writing. In 2026, the businesses winning in content are the ones producing material their competitors have not covered. Use People Also Ask scrapers to find the specific questions your customers are asking, then answer them directly and thoroughly.

Refresh Existing Content Regularly

Publish date is a major ranking signal. Update your best-performing pages — especially those already driving leads or carrying backlinks — and change the publication date. One blog post jumped from position 4 to #1 through a content refresh alone.

Press Releases Are Effective Again

New testing shows press releases now deliver measurable results across three areas: Local Pack rankings, organic SERP positions, and AI Overviews. In one case, a landing page saw an 83% traffic increase within 28 days. In another, Google's AI Overview directly quoted language from the release.

Location Pages for Multi-Location Businesses

Templated location pages get caught by Google's duplicate content filter. To make each page rank, you need genuinely unique content on every one.

Integrate Customer Testimonials

Use automated review collection and enhance your responses with location-specific keywords ("Thanks for choosing us for auto insurance in Denver!"). One insurance brand dominated branded searches by occupying 6–9 of the top results using this approach.

Write Unique Driving Directions

Create written directions to each office — do not rely solely on embedded maps. This provides naturally unique content with built-in local signals.

Add Detailed Employee Bios

Include expertise areas and service specializations for each location. This builds trust and creates naturally distinct page content.

Optimize Your Photos

Use descriptive filenames (replace IMG_4521.jpg with jakes-fireworks-kentwood-mi.jpg), add city-keyword alt text, and include multiple image types: interior, exterior, parking, staff, products, and neighborhood shots.

On-Page and Technical SEO

Use ALL CAPS Strategically in Title Tags

Major players like Yelp and TripAdvisor have done this for years. Capitalizing power words like BEST creates visual contrast on crowded SERPs, grabbing attention even from lower positions.

Go Long With Title Tags

Tests show that extremely long titles (over 200 characters) can increase visibility by 15%. Append neighborhood names to the end of your title tags to rank for a wider array of local keywords. When one business reverted to short titles, rankings dropped — restoring the long titles brought them back up. Specificity wins over brevity in local search.

Internal Linking

Link from your strongest pages (those with traffic and backlinks) to weaker ones — this transfers ranking power directly. One lawyer saw an immediate ranking improvement from a single strategic internal link. Use descriptive anchor text instead of "click here," keep the quantity reasonable, and connect blog posts to related service pages.

Simplify Your Navigation Menu

Overloaded menus confuse both users and Google. One client saw a 40% traffic increase after streamlining navigation; another saw 89% more traffic with a 25% conversion improvement.

Optimize for Mobile With Sticky Navigation

Keep contact information visible while scrolling. Research shows sticky menus increase goal conversions by 39% and session duration by 11%.

Optimize for "Near Me" Explicitly

Despite Google's AI understanding location context, explicitly including "near me" keywords on your website still significantly boosts organic rankings. Even the BBB and Google itself use these terms. For small businesses, this provides an essential edge when customers are searching for immediate services.

Format Phone Numbers With Parentheses

Google's AI Overviews can be surprisingly sensitive to formatting. In one case, Google displayed a competitor's phone number because the client's number lacked parentheses around the area code. Simply adding parentheses corrected the error. Small formatting details can dictate whether AI tools present your business or a rival's contact info.

Service Area Pages

Service area pages (SAPs) remain vital for service-based businesses that need to rank in cities where they have no physical location. Pack these pages with local landmarks, neighborhood names, and details about past jobs in that area to signal trusted local expertise.

An ENT doctor saw massive ranking improvements after creating SAPs focused on specific neighborhoods. These pages are essential for any business that travels to customers rather than operating from a storefront.

Visual Content

Infographics are the top-performing image type for small businesses. A study of 383 pages receiving Google image traffic found infographics outperformed flashy or trendy images for both clicks and conversions.

Why Infographics Work

Whether it is a lawyer mapping statistics by state, an electrician explaining safety protocols, or a dentist illustrating dental procedures — these visuals simplify complex topics. They are highly shareable and provide the clarity users crave. Focus on practical designs: data comparisons, process explanations, and simplified technical breakdowns rather than trendy or generic graphics.

Google Ads Are Essential

Google is moving decisively toward pay-to-play. Organic listings are losing features like call buttons that ads now carry prominently. Businesses running Google Ads are seeing better ROI than previously thanks to improved ad placement and richer features.

Run all ad types available to you — this is no longer optional for businesses that want to maintain visibility as organic real estate shrinks.

Long-Term Positioning

Shift Expertise-Building to Social Media

If you produce informational content to establish authority, YouTube and Reddit are where that effort pays off long-term. As search becomes dominated by AI-generated answers, platform-native content on these channels builds audiences that you own.

Monitor AI Traffic Sources

ChatGPT and similar tools are not major traffic drivers for small businesses yet, but that could change within a year. Track how much traffic AI platforms send you now so you can spot the shift early.

Open More Locations

If your single GBP listing is ranking well but traffic from it is declining — a trend accelerating in 2026 — the most direct countermeasure is expanding your physical footprint. More locations mean more listings and broader geographic coverage.

Use grid-based ranking tools like Places Scout or LocalFalcon to assess your current visibility across different areas of your city. Compare against competitors, identify where you have room to grow, and audit regularly. Small, targeted changes — a category fix, a content refresh, a single internal link — often create significant ranking improvements. The businesses that win in local SEO are the ones that audit consistently and adapt fast.
